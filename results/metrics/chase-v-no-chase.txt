+ fewer dram transactions for compressed variants -> compression at work
+ both small and large grids, no-chase -> more transactions in L2 and DRAM, because more neighbors are loaded; fwer in L1; small cache can't hold all neighbors
+ L2 hit rate drops dramatically for 512x512x64 -n from 512x512x64, but actually increases for 128x128x64 -n from 128x128x64!
#+ chase variants: much higher number of stalls due to memory dependencies (pointer chasing)
#  + in small grid: leads to lower issue slot utilization (28% for chasing vs 31% for non-chasing, as occupancy is around 0.86)
#  + in large grid: latency can be hidden, chase actually even has slightly larger issue slot utilization (22% vs 23%, high occupancy for both around 0.9)
